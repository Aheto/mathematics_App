1. What Is a Relation or Pattern?

A relation shows how two sets of values are connected through a consistent rule. Patterns can be:

  • Symbolic: Visual arrangements like matchstick shapes
  • Numerical: Ordered lists of numbers following a rule
‚úÖ The goal is to find the rule, extend the pattern, and explain the change between consecutive elements.

2. Extending Symbolic Patterns (Matchstick Example)

Example: Matchstick Sequence
- Pattern 1: 8 sticks
- Pattern 2: 15 sticks

Step 1: Observe the change
- From Pattern 1 ‚Üí 2: 15 - 8 = 7 sticks added

Step 2: Assume constant increase
- Pattern 3: 15 + 7 = 22
- Pattern 4: 22 + 7 = 29
- Pattern 5: 29 + 7 = 36

Step 3: Complete the table

Pattern No. 1 2 3 4 5 6 7
Number of Sticks 8 15 22 29 36 43 50

‚úÖ Rule: Each new pattern adds 7 matchsticks.

üí¨ Explanation: ‚ÄúEach pattern differs from the preceding one by 7 additional matchsticks.‚Äù

3. Extending Numerical Relations

Example: Number Sequence Table
Given:

Domain (x) 1 2 3 4 5 6 7
Co-domain (y) 4 7 10 ? 16 ? ?

Step 1: Find the common difference
- 7 - 4 = 3
- 10 - 7 = 3 ‚Üí constant increase of 3

Step 2: Fill in missing values
- After 10: 10 + 3 = 13
- After 13: 13 + 3 = 16 (matches given)
- Next: 16 + 3 = 19, then 22

Completed Table:

Domain (x) 1 2 3 4 5 6 7
Co-domain (y) 4 7 10 13 16 19 22

‚úÖ Rule: ‚ÄúEach output increases by 3 as the input increases by 1.‚Äù
