1. What Is the Highest Common Factor (HCF)?

The HCF of two or more numbers is the largest number that divides all of them exactly (with no remainder).

Example:
Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12
Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18
Common factors: 1, 2, 3, 6 ‚Üí HCF = 6

But for large numbers, listing factors is inefficient. Instead, we use prime factorization with powers.

3. Why This Method Works

  • The HCF must divide both numbers, so it can only contain primes common to both.
  • It cannot have a higher power than appears in either number‚Äîotherwise, it wouldn‚Äôt divide evenly.
  • Taking the lowest power ensures divisibility and maximizes the factor.
