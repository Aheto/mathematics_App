1. Understanding Significant Figures (SF) in Decimals

Significant figures are the digits in a number that carry meaningful information about its precision.

Rules for Identifying Significant Figures:

  • All non-zero digits are significant.
    ‚Üí e.g., 7.429 has 4 sf
  • Zeros between non-zero digits are significant.
    ‚Üí e.g., 7.021 has 4 sf
  • Leading zeros (before the first non-zero digit) are NOT significant.
    ‚Üí e.g., 0.0045 has 2 sf
  • Trailing zeros in a decimal number ARE significant.
    ‚Üí e.g., 0.360 has 3 sf (the final zero shows precision to thousandths)

2. Rounding to Significant Figures (SF)

Steps:

  1. Count the required number of significant digits from the first non-zero digit.
  2. Look at the next digit (to the right).

    • If <5 ‚Üí keep unchanged

3. Rounding to Decimal Places (DP)

Decimal places count digits after the decimal point, regardless of significance.

Steps:

  1. Count the required number of digits after the decimal.
  2. Look at the next digit.
    • If ‚â•5 ‚Üí round up
    • If <5 ‚Üí leave unchanged
  3. Drop all further digits.
