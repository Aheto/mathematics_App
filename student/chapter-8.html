1. Partitioning (Expanded Form) for Addition

Partitioning means breaking numbers into their place values before adding.

Example: Add 785 + 9,342
Step 1: Write in expanded form:
‚Äì 785 = 700 + 80 + 5
‚Äì 9,342 = 9,000 + 300 + 40 + 2

‚Äì Thousands: 9,000
‚Äì Hundreds: 700 + 300 = 1,000
‚Äì Tens: 80 + 40 = 120

4. Why Partitioning Works

  • Makes carrying (addition) and borrowing (subtraction) transparent.
  • Builds foundation for algebraic thinking and mental math.
