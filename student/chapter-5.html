1. What Are Powers of 10?

Powers of 10 are numbers like:

  • Positive exponents:
    10^1 = 10
    10^2 = 100
    10^3 = 1,000
    10^6 = 1,000,000
  • Negative exponents (for decimals):
    10^-1 = 0.1
    10^-2 = 0.01
    10^-3 = 0.001
‚úÖ Rule: The exponent tells you how many places to move the decimal point.

2. Multiplying by Powers of 10

  • When multiplying by 10^n (n > 0): Move the decimal point n places to the right.
  • When multiplying by 10^-n (n > 0): Move the decimal point n places to the left.
Examples:
‚Äì 45.67 √ó 10^2 = 45.67 √ó 100 = 4,567
‚Äì 45.67 √ó 10^-2 = 45.67 √ó 0.01 = 0.4567
