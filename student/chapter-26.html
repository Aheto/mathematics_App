1. What Are Equivalent Ratios?

Equivalent ratios express the same relationship between two (or more) quantities. They are formed by multiplying or dividing both parts of a ratio by the same non-zero number.

‚úÖ Example:
The ratio 36 : 48 : 24 can be simplified by dividing all terms by 12:
36 √∑ 12 = 3,   48 √∑ 12 = 4,   24 √∑ 12 = 2
So, 36:48:24 = 3:4:2

All of these are equivalent:

  • 36:48:24
  • 18:24:12
  • 9:12:6
  • 3:4:2

3. Writing Ratios as Common Fractions

In a two-quantity context, ratios can be written as fractions to show part-to-part or part-to-whole relationships.

Example: In a paint mix, red : blue = 3:5
- Red as fraction of total = 3/(3+5) = 3/8
- Blue as fraction of total = 5/8

This is useful when scaling recipes or budget allocations.

5. Key Features of Proportional Relationships

  • The ratio between corresponding quantities is constant.
  • You can always find a scale factor that links any two rows in the table.
  • When graphed, proportional pairs lie on a straight line through the origin (0,0).
